Visual Arts Building

The Visual Arts Building, designed by Steven Holl Architects in collaboration with BNIM Architects, provides the University of Iowa's School of Art & Art History 126,000 square feet of loft-like spaces for the departments of ceramics, sculpture, metals, photography, printmaking, and 3D multimedia. The building replaces the original arts building from 1936 that was heavily damaged during the flood of 2008. This new building is both vertically porous and volumetrically composed, allowing maximum interaction between departments within the social circulation spaces.
